Information about this app

This HTML/JS application is written by Boshuo Wang and Lari M. Koponen based on the following manuscript.

  • Boshuo Wang, Angel V. Peterchev, and Stefan M. Goetz. Three Novel Methods for Determining Motor Threshold with Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation Outperform Conventional Procedures. bioRxiv. DOI: 10.1101/2022.06.26.495134

Motor threshold (MT) is estimated using a stochastic root-finding method with a first-order digital control sequence (DCS1) or analog control sequence (ACS1). The control sequence follows harmonic (H) convergence with or without adaptive (A) stepping.

For the method to work correctly, it is important to start from an amplitude close to the MT and preferably suprathreshold (i.e., an amplitude that produces motor responses, more often than not). The amplitude used to locate the motor hot spot is a good starting point. With a good starting amplitude, the estimator takes 20 pulses to determine the MT.

All processing is done by the app locally. No data is shared online.

Known limitations and bugs:

  • The tool is provided as is. In particular, the automated stopping is based on an approximate algorithm which may prematurely declare the MT estimation “good”. The final judgement on the MT must always be made by the operator!
  • The algorithm may attempt to step to impossible amplitudes for the MT estimate (below 0% or above 100% MSO). This app limits both the MT estimate and the next pulse amplitude on the lower end, whereas only the next pulse amplitude is limited on the upper end, with the MT estimate possibly exceeding 100% MSO.
  • Copy to clipboard button does not work on some mobile devices. If the copy button tells that it has failed, please copy the text manually.
